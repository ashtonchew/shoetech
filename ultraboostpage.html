Adidas Boost

Boost is a material branded and used by Adidas, in the form of pellets which are compressed and molded for various shoes the company creates.
The pellets consist of proprietary thermoplastic urethane that are extruded and/or molded into a small pill shape. Adidas collaborated with the
German company BASF to create this material. This material is claimed to be particularly comfortable on the wearer's feet.

Boost had been revealed in 2012 when Adidas held a global event to unveil this new technology in a shoe called 'Energy Boost'.
This technology used the material in the shoe TPU for better energy transfer. A famous rapper named, Kanye West,
had seen all the boost shoes and were very impressed and so he had collaborated with Adidas in making one of the
most popular sneakers to date the Adidas 'Yeezy'. Kanye West first released the Adidas 750 Boost 'Light Brown'
which could have been bought for $350 retail is now sold on reselling platforms with the resell price tag of over $1700 today.
