Nike Air

Nike Air Max is a line of shoes first released by Nike in 1987. The shoe was originally designed by Tinker Hatfield, who started out working for
Nike as an architect designing shops; he also designed many of the Air Jordan models.The Nike air technology was created and patented by
employee M. Frank Rudy. The Nike Air Max shoe uses a large air cushioning unit at the heel which is visible from the side of the midsole in
most models. Types of Air Max cushioning include: 180 Air in the early 1990s which was visible through the bottom of the shoe
(although smaller portions of Air units are visible through the bottom of many Air Max models)

• "Air Max2" which does not have the holes in the cushioning unit and is of high pressure,
• "Tube Air" which is visible in several small circles on the midsole of the shoe,
• "Total Air" which is another word for full Air Max cushioning,
• "Tuned Air," which is a system of individual pods tuned to different areas of the foot.

FUN FACT! :

In the mid-1990s the line, especially the Air Max 95 model, experienced such a surge of popularity in Japan that it led to
a phenomenon known as "Air Max hunting". The extremely inflated prices of the shoes led to a rash of muggings in the
normally-peaceful country wherein Air Max wearers were attacked and their shoes were stolen. Even used shoes were in
demand, and fakes/replicas also became a serious problem.
